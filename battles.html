Beowulf and Grendel

Grendel, a monster who has been terrorizing the Danes at their great mead-hall Heorot, is confronted and defeated by Beowulf, who fights Grendel bare-handed and eventually tears off one of Grendel's arms which forced Grendel to retreat back to his lair. Grendel laid in his layer bleeding to death, but eventually, Beowulf finishes him off when he meets Grendel in his layer. Gruesome note: Beowulf hung Grendel's arm in Heorot and they held a party dancing and drinking as the arm just hung there.

Beowulf and Grendel's Mother

Seeking revenge for her son's death, Grendel's mother attacks Heorot. Beowulf dives into her underwater lair where he engages in a fierce battle with Grendel's mother and kills her with a sword forged by giants, once again saving the Danes from disaster.
